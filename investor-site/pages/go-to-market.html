Go-to-Market Plan

Systematic customer acquisition with measured channel economics

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Hotel Consulting ICP

  • Property type: Independent and lifestyle hotels
  • Size: 80-250 keys
  • RevPAR range: $80-200
  • Ownership: Owner-led or institutional with active asset management
  • Geography: Primary and secondary markets in [Target Regions]
  • Pain points: Margin pressure, labor costs, OTA dependency

Export Company ICP

  • Company size: Mid-market buyers ($10M-500M revenue)
  • Order profile: Repeat orders, $100K-1M per shipment
  • Industries: Consumer goods, industrial supplies, electronics
  • Credit profile: Creditworthy with established operations
  • Geography: [Target Import Markets]
  • Pain points: Supply reliability, cash flow, compliance complexity

Sales Pipeline Process

Stage Activities Exit Criteria Typical Duration Conversion Rate
1. Discover Initial outreach, qualification call BANT confirmed, meeting scheduled 1-3 days 20%
2. Diagnose Discovery meeting, data review Pain quantified, champion identified 5-7 days 60%
3. Proposal Custom proposal, ROI analysis Proposal presented to decision makers 3-5 days 80%
4. Pilot Scoped pilot or proof of concept Success metrics achieved 30-60 days 70%
5. Land Contract negotiation, onboarding Contract signed, kickoff scheduled 5-10 days 90%
6. Expand Upsell, cross-sell, referrals Additional services or properties Ongoing 40%

Overall Funnel Math:

100 Leads → 20 Discoveries → 12 Diagnoses → 9.6 Proposals → 6.7 Pilots → 6 Lands → 2.4 Expansions

Lead-to-Customer Rate: 6%

Messaging Pillars

1. Measurable Outcomes

"We guarantee specific, measurable improvements in your key metrics - not just recommendations in a PowerPoint."

  • 15%+ RevPAR lift in 6 weeks
  • 20%+ GOP improvement in 90 days
  • ROI documented and verified

2. Weekly Cadence

"See progress every week, not every quarter. Our rapid iteration model ensures you're never waiting for results."

  • Weekly dashboards and updates
  • Real-time performance tracking
  • Immediate course corrections

3. Cash Discipline

"Every decision optimizes for cash flow and working capital - because cash is oxygen for your business."

  • Positive cash impact from day one
  • Working capital optimization
  • Insured and managed risk
