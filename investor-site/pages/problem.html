Problem & Opportunity

Two massive markets with clear gaps and urgent needs

Hotel Consulting Company

Market Gaps

Under-optimized RevPAR

Hotels leave 15-25% of potential revenue on the table through poor pricing strategies and channel mix management.

Weak Mix Management

Limited understanding of customer segments and inefficient allocation across channels leads to margin erosion.

Fragmented Tech Stacks

Average hotel uses 8-12 different systems that don't communicate, creating data silos and operational inefficiencies.

Slow Pricing Cadence

Manual rate updates 1-2x daily vs. algorithmic pricing that adjusts every 15 minutes based on demand signals.

High Labor Costs

Labor represents 45-55% of hotel operating costs, with significant inefficiencies in scheduling and task allocation.

Customer Pain

Hotel owners and operators face a critical challenge: they need to increase NOI (Net Operating Income) and implement credible controls without adding fixed SG&A expenses. Traditional consulting firms offer expensive, long-term engagements that rarely deliver measurable results.

Urgency

Rising costs combined with flattening growth rates make flow-through discipline essential. Hotels that don't optimize now risk permanent market share loss to more sophisticated competitors.

Export Company

Market Gaps

Trust Deficit

Buyers lack trusted sourcing partners with end-to-end quality control and transparent pricing.

Limited Finance Options

Traditional trade finance requires established relationships and collateral many mid-market buyers lack.

Opaque Landed Costs

Hidden fees, duties, and freight charges make budgeting impossible and erode margins.

Customer Pain

Buyers face volatile freight rates, customs complexity, and credit risk that block orders. They need reliable supply chains with predictable cash conversion cycles but lack the scale to negotiate favorable terms.

Urgency

Supply chain disruptions and geopolitical tensions have made reliable sourcing a C-suite priority. Companies that can't secure stable supply risk losing customers and market position.
