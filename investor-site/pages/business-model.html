Business Model & Unit Economics

Multiple revenue streams with attractive unit economics and cash profiles

Revenue Streams

Hotel Consulting Revenue

  • Fixed-fee sprints ($50k-$200k)
  • Success fees (10-20% of incremental GOP)
  • Monthly retainers ($10k-$25k)
  • Training workshops ($5k-$15k)

Export Company Revenue

  • Sourcing fees (2-5% of order value)
  • Freight orchestration fees ($500-$2k)
  • Margin on goods (10-15%)
  • Finance spreads (1-2% monthly)
  • Insurance facilitation (0.5-1%)

Cost Structure

Direct Costs

  • Consultant/analyst salaries
  • Travel and accommodation
  • Technology and data subscriptions
  • Quality inspections
  • Broker and forwarder fees

Indirect Costs

  • Sales and marketing
  • Compliance and legal
  • Insurance premiums
  • Office and administration
  • Training and development

Working Capital Analysis

Cash Cycle Components:

• Days Sales Outstanding (DSO): 60 days

• Days Payable Outstanding (DPO): 30 days

• Inventory Days: 25 days

• Cash Gap = DSO - DPO + Inventory = 55 days

Working Capital Calculation:

• Sales × Cash Gap ÷ 365 = $250,000 × 55 ÷ 365 = $37,671

• Finance cost at 1.5%/month for 1.5 months = 2.25% of sales

Key Business Metrics

Hotel Consulting Formulas

RevPAR = ADR × Occupancy
Annual Rooms Revenue ≈ RevPAR × 365 × Rooms
ΔRevenue = New RevPAR − Old RevPAR
Flow-through % = ΔGOP ÷ ΔRevenue
Payback (months) = CAC ÷ Monthly Contribution per Client

Export Company Formulas

Export Landed Cost = COGS + Freight + Duty + Insurance + Broker + QC
Cash Gap (days) = DSO − DPO + Inventory Days
Working Capital Need ≈ Sales × Cash Gap ÷ 365
Financing Cost ≈ Sales × Rate_per_month × (CashGap/30)
Insured Loss Expectation ≈ Exposure × Default_Prob × (1 − Coverage%)
