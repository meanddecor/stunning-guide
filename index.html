Product Vision: Restaurant Management System

A custom restaurant management system—OpenTable-style plus CRM—capturing guest data, preventing no-shows, enabling cross-venue marketing, and optimizing table turnover across all APM properties.

Must Have (MVP)

Outcome-driven features finalized in Discovery phase

  • Unified guest profiles: Read from reservation/POS exports or APIs
  • Consent capture & audit: Email/SMS per venue + global unsubscribe
  • Deposits/no-show guard: Stripe Checkout hosted (PCI SAQ-A scope - no raw card data on our servers)
  • Two-way SMS/email: Confirmations, reminders, waitlist notifications
    Marketing SMS requires seller/venue-specific prior express written consent; we store auditable opt-ins per venue and honor STOP/HELP. TCPA consent + A2P 10DLC registration required. The FCC's 2023 'one-to-one consent' lead-gen rule was vacated by the Eleventh Circuit (Jan 2025) and the FCC repealed it (July 2025). It is not in force. We still require venue-specific, documented consent for any marketing SMS.
  • One cross-venue campaign: With holdout group & revenue attribution
  • Manager daily digest: Covers, no-shows, deposits, KPI dashboard
TCPA/CAN-SPAM compliant. Venue-level consent, per-channel opt-in, single "unsubscribe from all venues" control. All marketing emails include clear opt-out honored within 10 business days. A2P 10DLC registration includes brand + campaign registration with carrier fee budgeting. Guest UIs follow WCAG 2.2 AA; ADA accessibility testing included in Beta.

Should Have (Pilot+)

Phase 2 enhancements based on pilot results

  • Additional campaigns: Two more cross-venue marketing campaigns

  • Multi-tenant SaaS: Focus on APM-specific optimization first
  • Mobile apps: Web-responsive interface sufficient for MVP

Scope creep: Freeze v1 scope, queue extras to v1.1, preserve timeline/cost

Accessibility gaps: Remediate to WCAG 2.2 AA, retest, add periodic scans

Deposit policy conversion impact: If deposit policy pushes conversion down, A/B test softer holds (card-on-file, 24-hr window) before rolling back globally
